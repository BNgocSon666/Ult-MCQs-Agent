Welcome! This system is designed to help you turn any document or audio file into a complete quiz (also called an "exam").

This guide is split into two parts:
1.  **For Creators:** The person who uploads files, creates questions, and builds exams (e.g., a teacher, a manager).
2.  **For Takers:** The person who receives a link to take an exam (e.g., a student, an employee, or a guest).

## üë®‚Äçüè´ Guide for Creators (Teachers/Admins)

This is your main control panel. Here‚Äôs how to go from a document to a shareable exam.

### 1. Getting Started (Sign Up & Log In)
First, you need an account.
* **Register:** On the login page, click "Sign up now". Enter your username, email, and password.
* **Log In:** Use your new account to log in. You will be taken to your main Dashboard.

### 2. Step 1: Create Questions (The AI Agent)
This is where you feed your content to the AI.
1.  From the Dashboard, you'll be on the **"Create (AI)"** tab.
2.  Click **"Choose File"** and upload your content. This can be a `.pdf`, `.docx`, `.txt`, or an audio file like `.mp3` or `.wav`.
3.  Choose the number of questions you want.
4.  Click **"Start Generation"**.

The system will now read your file and use AI to generate questions.

### 3. Step 2: Review and Edit the AI's Work
A pop-up window will appear with the AI's results.
1.  **Check the Source:** A blue notice box will tell you if the questions were based on the **full document** or a **summary** (which the AI creates for long files).
2.  **Review Questions:** Scroll down to see the questions, the correct answer, and the AI's quality score (e.g., "97 points").
3.  **Edit (Important):** If you see a mistake (a typo, a wrong answer, or a confusing question), click the **"Edit"** button on that question. You can rewrite the question, change the options, and select the correct answer yourself.
4.  **Save to Library:** Once you are happy with the questions, click the big **"Save to Library"** button at the bottom. This saves all the questions (including your edits) to your personal collection.

### 4. Step 3: Manage Your Question Library
This is your personal "bank" of all the questions you've saved.
1.  Click the **"Question Library"** tab at the top of the Dashboard.
2.  Here you will see all your saved questions.
3.  **Filter & Search:** Use the "Filter Bar" at the top to find specific questions. You can search by keyword, filter by the file you uploaded, or sort by score.
4.  **Pagination:** If you have many questions, use the "1, 2, 3..." buttons at the bottom to navigate pages.
5.  **Manage:** You can **"Edit"** or **"Delete"** any question from your library here.

### 5. Step 4: Build an Exam
Now you can combine your questions into a formal exam.
1.  Click the **"My Exams"** tab.
2.  Click the **"+ Create New Exam"** button.
3.  **Fill in Details:** Give your exam a "Title" (e.g., "Midterm Quiz") and a "Description".
4.  **Select Questions:** Your entire Question Library will appear. **Check the box** next to every question you want to include in this exam.
5.  Click **"Save Exam"**.

### 6. Step 5: Share and See Results
Your exam is now ready to be shared.
1.  On the "My Exams" page, click the **card (tile) of the exam** you just created.
2.  This takes you to the **Exam Detail Page**. On this page, you can:
    * **Share:** Click **"Copy Link"**. Send this link to your students or anyone who needs to take the exam.
    * **Export:** Click **"Export to PDF"** to download a PDF copy of the exam (with answers included) for your own records.
    * **View Results:** Click **"View Results"**. A window will pop up showing a table of everyone who has taken your exam, when they took it, and what their score was.

## üë©‚Äçüéì Guide for Takers (Students/Guests)

This is your guide if you have received a link to take an exam.

### 1. Starting the Exam
1.  Open the link (e.g., `.../take/abc123...`) your teacher or manager sent you.
2.  You will see the **Exam Start Page** with the exam's title.
3.  **Enter Your Name:** If you are not logged in, you must enter your name in the text box (e.g., "John Smith").
4.  Click **"Start Exam"**.

### 2. Taking the Exam
You will now see the exam questions.
1.  **Read Carefully:** Read each question.
2.  **Check for Context:** If a question says **"Based on the text below:"**, a short paragraph will appear above it. You *must* read this text to find the correct answer.
3.  **Select Your Answer:** Click on the option (A, B, C, or D) you believe is correct.
4.  **Submit:** When you have answered all questions, click the **"Submit Exam"** button at the top or bottom of the page.

### 3. Seeing Your Results
You will immediately be taken to the results page.
* **Your Score:** You will see your total score at the top (e.g., "8 / 10").
* **Detailed Review:** Scroll down to see every question:
    * **Correct** answers are marked with a **green border**.
    * **Incorrect** answers are marked with a **red border**. The page will show you the wrong answer you picked and also highlight the correct answer you missed.
